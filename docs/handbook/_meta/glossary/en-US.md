### Append-only

**From [Ledger](/admin/handbook/tools/financial/ledger):**

Write strategy where existing records are never modified or deleted; correction comes through new compensating records.

### Audit trail

**From [Ledger](/admin/handbook/tools/financial/ledger):**

Immutable chronological history of all changes, used for audit and state reconstruction.

### Compensating entry

**From [Ledger](/admin/handbook/tools/financial/ledger):**

A new entry created to neutralize the effect of a previous entry; the original entry stays intact.

### Double-entry

**From [Ledger](/admin/handbook/tools/financial/ledger):**

Accounting model where every movement is recorded simultaneously as a debit and a credit in different accounts, summing to zero.

### Idempotency key

**From [Ledger](/admin/handbook/tools/financial/ledger):**

Key uniquely identifying a request; lets retries be safe without duplicating effect.

### Journal entry

**From [Ledger](/admin/handbook/tools/financial/ledger):**

Atomic unit of financial movement — one entry groups 2+ lines that balance per currency.

### Journal line

**From [Ledger](/admin/handbook/tools/financial/ledger):**

Individual component of an entry — binds an account, direction (D/C), amount, and currency.

### Money tuple

**From [Ledger](/admin/handbook/tools/financial/ledger):**

Representation `(amountCents: bigint, currency)` — never a bare `number` or `Decimal`.

### postedAt

**From [Ledger](/admin/handbook/tools/financial/ledger):**

Chronology timestamp of an entry; used for point-in-time balances and statement ordering (not `createdAt`).

### Posting

**From [Ledger](/admin/handbook/tools/financial/ledger):**

Action of recording an entry into the ledger via `postJournalEntry`; the only authorized write path.

### Reversal

**From [Ledger](/admin/handbook/tools/financial/ledger):**

Compensating entry with directions flipped that neutralizes the effect of an original entry.

### Source kind/id

**From [Ledger](/admin/handbook/tools/financial/ledger):**

Polymorphic reference linking an entry back to the business event that produced it (charge, commission, etc.).
